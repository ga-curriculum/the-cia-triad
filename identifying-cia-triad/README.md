**Learning objective:** By the end of this lesson, students will be able to identify the CIA triad in real life scenarios.

Now that we understand the components of the CIA Triad, let's analyze some real-world examples to see how they come into play.

## Scenario 1: Data Breach at a Healthcare Provider

Imagine a healthcare provider experiences a data breach where patient records are accessed by unauthorized individuals.

- **Confidentiality:** Only authorized healthcare providers should have access patient information. Unauthorized individuals obtaining access immediately breaches confidentiality requirements.

- **Integrity:** Medical records must be accurate and unaltered. Depending on the level of access the unauthorized individuals obtain, they may have the ability to alter records.

- **Availability:** Critical information must be accessible during emergencies, which could be impacted if the unauthorized individuals utilize ransomware.

## Scenario 2: Ransomware Attack on a Financial Institution

Consider a ransomware attack on a financial institution where attackers encrypt critical data and demand a ransom.

- **Confidentiality:** Ransomware encryption prevents *anyone* from seeing the data. If the data was encrypted already, the attackers wouldn't be able to see the data, so this may not cause a breach in confidentiality.

- **Integrity:** Your balance and account information must be accurate for integrity to be present. If the attack changes anything with the data or your account suddenly says $0.00 because of it, integrity is breached.

- **Availability:** You need access to your money 24/7. Unless there are controls in place and the bank has backups, this type of event will likely prevent you from accessing your money immediately. This negates the availability.

## Scenario 3: Website Defacement

Suppose a company's website is defaced by hackers who replace the original content with malicious or misleading information. This primarily impacts the integrity of the website, as the content is no longer accurate or trustworthy. It may also affect availability if the defacement renders the website inaccessible to users.

- **Confidentiality:** If there was anything confidential on the website, confidentiality may have been impacted in the process of the attack.

- **Integrity:** Since the content is no longer accurate or trustworthy, this attack mainly effects the integrity of the website.

- **Availability:** Availability would be impacted if the defacement renders the website inaccessible to users.

## Protecting CIA: Practical Strategies

To safeguard the CIA Triad, organizations can implement various practical measures in their IT environments. Let's explore some strategies for each aspect.

### Protecting Confidentiality

- Implement strong access controls and authentication mechanisms to ensure only authorized users can access sensitive data.

- Use encryption techniques to protect data at rest and in transit, making it unreadable to unauthorized parties.

- Conduct regular security awareness training for employees to educate them on handling sensitive information securely.

### Protecting Integrity

- Implement data validation and input sanitization techniques to prevent unauthorized modifications to data.

- Use digital signatures and hashing algorithms to verify the authenticity and integrity of data.

- Establish version control and change management processes to track and audit modifications to critical data.

### Protecting Availability

- Implement redundancy and failover mechanisms to ensure continuous availability of critical systems and data.

- Conduct regular backup and disaster recovery testing to minimize downtime in the event of a disruption.

- Monitor system performance and capacity to proactively identify and address potential availability issues.
